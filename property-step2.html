Step 2: Viewing the Property

If an in-person visit isn't possible, request a live video walkthrough. Use the following checklist to evaluate the unit:

  • Total Monthly Cost: Confirm which utilities (water, gas, electric, trash) are covered by the landlord and which are your responsibility.
  • Upfront Costs: Clarify the security deposit, application fees, and any brokerage costs.
  • Surroundings: Check Google Street View for nearby construction that might cause noise or dust.
  • The "Living Check": Test water pressure, light switches, and outlets. Note that many U.S. bedrooms do not have ceiling lights; you will likely need floor lamps.
  • Noise Awareness: Listen for building boilers or street noise, especially in top-floor units or those facing busy avenues.

Note for New York Renters: NYC has "Rent Stabilized" and "Rent Controlled" units with legal limits on rent increases. Other units are "Unregulated." You can verify a building's status via the New York State Division of Housing and Community Renewal (DHCR).
