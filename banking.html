Banking & Credit

1. Opening an Account

You do not need a Social Security Number (SSN) to open a bank account. Many institutions accept an ITIN or a foreign passport.

Required: Proof of identity (passport/IDNYC), proof of address (utility bill/lease), and an initial deposit ($25-$100).

2. Understanding Credit Scores (300-850)

A credit score is a number between 300 and 850. Since you are new to the country, you likely have "no credit," which can be just as difficult as "bad credit."

Score Range What it Means Impact on You
740 – 850 Excellent Easy to get loans; lowest interest rates.
670 – 739 Good Most landlords will accept you; standard rates.
580 – 669 Fair May require a higher security deposit for apartments.
300 – 579 Poor Very hard to get approved for credit or housing.

3. Building Credit from Zero

Building credit takes time, but you can start on Day 1 using these three tools:

  • Secured Credit Cards: A card backed by a cash deposit that reports to credit bureaus.
  • Credit-Builder Loans: Small loans held in savings that build payment history as you "pay them back."
  • Rent Reporting: Services that add your on-time rent payments to your credit file.

⚠️ The Two Golden Rules of Credit

  1. Pay on time, every time: Even one late payment can tank your score by 100 points. Set up "Auto-Pay" for everything.
  2. Don't "Max Out" your cards: Lenders like to see that you use less than 30% of your available credit. If your limit is $1,000, try to never let your balance go above $300.
