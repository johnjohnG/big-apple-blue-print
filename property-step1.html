Step 1: Finding your home - The Search

Define Your Priorities

Before starting, categorize your needs based on budget and lifestyle. Key factors often include:

  • Living Situation: Choosing between a room in a shared apartment, a studio, or a full house.
  • Transportation: Proximity to public transit vs. the need for a dedicated parking space.
  • Amenities: Weighing the value of building perks (gym, pool, laundry) against monthly costs.

Apartment vs. House

  • Apartments: Often more affordable and easier to maintain. Building management typically handles repairs like plumbing or heating issues.
  • Houses: Offer more space, privacy, and outdoor areas, but tenants are often responsible for upkeep, such as lawn mowing in summer or snow shoveling and pipe protection in winter.

Where to Search

The most common marketplace for U.S. rentals is Zillow, which features everything from studios to houses, often including 3D virtual tours.

Additional Resources:

  • Rooms/Roommates: Local Facebook or WhatsApp groups, Roomster, Diggz, and Nooklyn.
  • General Listings: Craigslist, Reddit, or university housing boards for students.
⚠️

Safety Note: Exercise caution on open platforms. Verify the identity of the lister by searching their phone number or professional history before sharing personal information or funds.
