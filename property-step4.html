Step 4: Legal Agreements

Signing the Lease

After the viewing and the application, the Lease Agreement is the final step. The lease is a binding legal contract. Ensure the following basics are correct:

  • Legal Identities: The full names of every adult living in the apartment and the legal name of the landlord (could be an LLC).
  • The Location: The full address, including the specific apartment or unit number.
  • The Timeline: Exact start and end dates (e.g., "September 1, 2024, to August 31, 2025").
  • Security Deposit: In NY, this is capped at one month's rent and must be returned within 14 days of move-out.
  • The Rent: The monthly rent amount, the day it is due (usually the 1st), and accepted payment methods (a check, etc.).
  • Included Services: A list of utilities the landlord pays for (e.g., "Heat and Hot Water included; Tenant pays Electricity and Gas").
  • Policies: Check for specific rules on pets, painting walls, or guest limits (often 15 days).

Understanding Brokerage Agreements

Before a broker starts showing you apartments, they may ask you to sign a representation agreement. You may also wish to hire a broker, and you will sign a Representation Agreement. Key clauses to watch for include:

  • Exclusivity: You may be barred from working with other brokers.
  • Protection Period: A fee may be due if you lease a building the broker showed you, even after the contract ends.
  • Dual Agency: A broker may represent both you and the landlord; ensure this is disclosed.
