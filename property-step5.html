Step 5: Move-In Logistics

Once the lease is signed, the focus shifts to logistics. Use this checklist to ensure you don't miss anything before you get the keys.

Utilities Setup: Call providers (like Con Edison for electricity/gas) to ensure service is transferred to your name starting on your move-in date. Do this at least one week in advance.

Get Renter's Insurance: Most New York landlords require proof of insurance before handing over the keys. It is a small monthly cost that protects your belongings and covers you for liability.

The Final Walkthrough: On move-in day, do a lap around the apartment. Test the faucets, light switches, and locks. Crucially, take photos of any existing damage (scratches on the floor, stains on the wall) and email them to your landlord immediately. This protects your security deposit later.

Pro-Tip: The "Move-In Inspection Form"

Some landlords will give you a pre-printed form to fill out. If they do, be very thorough. If they don't give you one, don't wait for them, create your own simple document or email thread. If you need a template, visit our Resources Page, where you'll find a printable pdf template for an Apartment Condition Report.

Building Logistics: If you are in a large building, ask the management if you need to reserve the elevator or provide a "Certificate of Insurance" (COI) from your moving company.

Change Your Address: Forward your mail via the USPS website and update your address with your bank, employer, and any subscription services.
